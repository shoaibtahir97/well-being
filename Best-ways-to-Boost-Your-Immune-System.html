Best ways to Boost Your Immune System - COVID-19

Bilal Khan Contributor

We are in the middle of a pandemic, the death tolls are on the rise, and a sense of panic prevails every individual. Many myths surround the Coronavirus, and this has resulted in a lot of fear generation among the masses. I've been getting a lot of questions about the best ways to boost the immune system. I'll be discussing different ways to boost the immune system. I don't recommend the usage of immune-boosting supplements. We'll keep it highly natural, which is the perfect way to go about this.
First, let us discuss the symptoms of Covid-19. The usual symptoms that most carriers experience are Cough, chills, shivering, Fever, joint pain, and nose dripping. Being a health care professional, people have asked a lot of questions. Do we need to panic?
Not at all!
Should you be cautious?
Absolutely!
However, the best way to tackle Coronavirus is to boost your immune system. Boosting the immune system helps one's body fight against the virus. You can consider it a war going inside your body; your antibodies are fighting against the foreign virus and preventing further infection. Immune system boosters improve the fighting capacity of your body and improve overall recovery. Individuals with a sound immune system may not even show symptoms because the virus couldn't penetrate their healthy body. Without further waiting, lets talk begin with immune system boosting techniques.

Immune system boosting foods

• Citrus fruits

Citrus fruits contain vitamin C, which is very important in the fight against infections. Fruits such as strawberries, oranges, and kiwis are essential and should be consumed often. Daily intake of citrus fruits keeps the body healthy and improves immunity in the longer term.

• Vegetables

Red bell peppers, green vegetables, ginger, spinach are examples of plants that improve the body's defense mechanism. Their consumption is essential and highly recommended daily. A balanced diet, however, will be far more beneficial to maintain health and fitness.

• The immune system tea

I've been drinking this mixture for a long time now. It's not a tea, and it doesn't contain caffeine. About 3 to 4 thin slices of lemon. Add them in an empty cup. Add about 125 ml of water in a container. Add about 6 to 10 Nigella sativa ( Kalonji ) seeds to this container. Now start heating the vessel till the water starts boiling. Pour the water into the cup containing the slices of lemons. Add two tablespoons of honey to this mixture and stir. I recommend biting into the seeds to extract out the nutrition. Honey is anti-bacterial, the lemon provides the recommended dose of vitamin C, and the Kalonji seeds contain thymoquinone, an anti-inflammatory agent that reduces redness and swelling. It gives relief for strep throat and sore throat and also boosts one's resistance against infections.

• Ascorbic Acid Tablets

Vitamin C tablets are easy to get access to and possess the recommended amount of vitamin C required daily by the body. However, the recommended dose for children differs from that of an adult. It is essential to keep that in mind as overdosing on vitamin C results in an upset stomach. However, no severe side effects are reported on its consumption.
Use the above health hacks, and you're destined to have a sound immune system in the longer term. Stay healthy, and let's defeat the pandemic!
